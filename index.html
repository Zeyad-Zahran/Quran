The most famous mosques

Admin
Al Madinah Mosque

The Prophet’s Mosque in Medina is one of the greatest and holiest mosques in Islam, ranking as the second most sacred site after the Grand Mosque in Mecca. It was founded by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ after his migration to Medina and served as a central hub for worship, education, and Islamic teachings. The mosque is renowned for its immense spiritual significance, particularly for housing Al-Rawdah Al-Sharifah, about which the Prophet ﷺ said: “Between my house and my pulpit is a garden from the gardens of Paradise.” This makes it a highly sought-after place for prayer among worshippers. The mosque’s architectural design is a stunning blend of traditional Islamic heritage and modern advancements.

Admin
Al-Masjid Al-Haram

Al-Masjid Al-Haram, also known as the Grand Mosque, is the holiest and most revered site in Islam, located in the sacred city of Mecca, Saudi Arabia. It surrounds the Kaaba, the most sacred structure in Islam, towards which millions of Muslims worldwide direct their prayers. The Grand Mosque is the largest mosque in the world and serves as the focal point of the annual Hajj pilgrimage, one of the Five Pillars of Islam, as well as Umrah, which can be performed at any time of the year. The mosque's architectural magnificence reflects its deep spiritual significance. It features towering minarets, vast marble courtyards, and state-of-the-art facilities to accommodate millions of worshippers. The Mataf, the area around the Kaaba where pilgrims perform Tawaf (circumambulation), is meticulously maintained to ensure a smooth and safe experience for pilgrims. The mosque also houses Maqam Ibrahim, the Well of Zamzam, and the sacred Hajar al-Aswad (Black Stone), all of which hold immense religious importance.

Admin
Al-Aqsa Mosque

Al-Aqsa Mosque, located in the Old City of Jerusalem, is one of the holiest sites in Islam and holds deep spiritual, historical, and cultural significance. It is the third holiest mosque in Islam, after Al-Masjid Al-Haram in Mecca and Al-Masjid An-Nabawi in Medina. The mosque is part of the larger Al-Haram Al-Sharif (Noble Sanctuary), which also includes the Dome of the Rock, an iconic golden-domed structure that is one of the most recognized landmarks in the world. Al-Aqsa Mosque is deeply rooted in Islamic history, as it was the first Qibla (direction of prayer) for Muslims before it was changed to the Kaaba in Mecca. Additionally, it is believed to be the site where Prophet Muhammad ﷺ ascended to the heavens during the Isra and Mi'raj (Night Journey and Ascension), making it a place of immense religious importance. The mosque’s architecture reflects a rich Islamic heritage, with beautiful arches, intricate calligraphy, and a grand prayer hall that can accommodate thousands of worshippers.

The most famous sheikhs

By Admin
Sheikh Yasser Al-Dosari

Sheikh Yasser bin Rashid Al-Dosari is one of the most renowned Quran reciters and an Imam of the Grand Mosque in Mecca. He is known for his soulful and melodious recitation, which has gained widespread recognition. He memorized the Quran at a young age and studied Islamic sciences. Before leading prayers at the Grand Mosque, he served as an Imam in several mosques. He also participates in religious lectures and events, contributing to Quranic education and Tajweed teaching. His recitations are broadcast on Islamic channels and radio stations and have a vast following online

By Admin
Sheikh Mohammed Mahmoud Al-Tablawi

Sheikh Mohammed Mahmoud Al-Tablawi (1934 – 2020) was one of the greatest Quran reciters in the Islamic world. He was known for his powerful voice and unique style, combining precision and deep spirituality, making him one of Egypt’s most distinguished reciters. He memorized the Quran at a young age and began his recitation journey early, eventually becoming an official reciter on Egyptian radio. His recordings gained widespread recognition, influencing generations of Quran reciters. Sheikh Al-Tablawi left a lasting Quranic legacy, and his recitations continue to be broadcast on various channels and radio stations, inspiring Muslims worldwide.

By Admin
Sheikh Mohammed Metwally Al-Sha'arawy

Sheikh Mohammed Metwally Al-Sha'arawy (1911 – 1998) was one of the most prominent scholars of Al-Azhar and one of the most famous Quran interpreters in modern times. He was known for his simple yet profound style of explaining the Quran, making his teachings accessible to people from all walks of life. Born in Egypt, he memorized the Quran at a young age and later joined Al-Azhar, where he became renowned for his knowledge and eloquence. He served in various religious positions, including as the Minister of Awqaf (Religious Endowments). He left a significant intellectual legacy through his Quranic interpretations and religious lectures, and his programs continue to be broadcast, influencing millions of Muslims around the world
